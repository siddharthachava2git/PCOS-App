What is PCOS?
Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal condition affecting 1 in 10 women. It causes irregular periods, excess male hormones (androgens), and sometimes small cysts on the ovaries. It affects your weight, skin, hair, fertility, and long-term heart health.
✅ Early lifestyle changes can dramatically reduce long-term risks
Insulin Resistance
When your body's cells don't respond well to insulin (the hormone that controls blood sugar), your pancreas makes extra insulin to compensate. This extra insulin raises male hormone levels, worsening PCOS. Most women with PCOS have some degree of insulin resistance — and lifestyle changes directly fix this.
🍽 Low-GI diet and regular exercise are the best natural treatments
HDL — "Good Cholesterol"
HDL (High-Density Lipoprotein) removes bad cholesterol from your arteries and protects your heart. Women with PCOS often have low HDL. A healthy HDL reading for women is above 50 mg/dL. Low HDL increases your risk of heart disease over time.
🏃 Regular exercise is one of the best ways to raise HDL naturally
Triglycerides — Blood Fats
Triglycerides are fats that circulate in your blood, derived from food you eat. Levels above 150 mg/dL are considered high and are linked to insulin resistance, obesity, and too much sugar. In PCOS, triglycerides tend to be elevated, increasing cardiovascular risk.
🥗 Cutting sugar and refined carbs quickly lowers triglycerides
Cardiometabolic Risk
Women with PCOS have a higher lifetime risk of heart disease, type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, and metabolic syndrome. This combined risk is called "cardiometabolic risk." The important news: lifestyle changes started now can cut this risk significantly.
❤️ What you do today matters enormously for your future health
LDL — "Bad Cholesterol"
LDL (Low-Density Lipoprotein) deposits cholesterol in artery walls, increasing heart attack and stroke risk. Ideal LDL for women with PCOS is below 100 mg/dL. A healthy diet, regular exercise, and medication (if needed) keep LDL in check.
🫒 Replace saturated fats with olive oil, nuts, and seeds
